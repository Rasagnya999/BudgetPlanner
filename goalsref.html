Goal Setting: Users can create various types of financial goals, such as saving for a vacation, buying a house, paying off debt, or building an emergency fund. They specify the target amount they want to achieve and the timeframe within which they aim to reach it.

Categorization: Goals can be categorized based on their purpose or priority, making it easier for users to organize and prioritize their financial objectives. Common categories may include savings goals, investment goals, debt repayment goals, etc.

Tracking Progress: The app provides visual representations of users' progress towards their goals, such as progress bars or charts. This helps users stay motivated and informed about how close they are to reaching their targets.

Automatic Contributions: Users can set up automatic contributions or transfers from their income or designated accounts towards their goals. This ensures consistent progress without the need for manual intervention.

Notifications and Reminders: The app may send notifications or reminders to users to keep them on track with their goals. These reminders can prompt users to make contributions, review their progress, or update their goals as needed.

Integration with Budgeting: Goals are often integrated seamlessly with the budgeting features of the app. Users can allocate funds from their budget towards their goals and track how these allocations affect their progress.

Goal Analysis: Advanced features may include goal analysis tools that provide insights into users' spending habits, income sources, and saving patterns to help them optimize their goal-setting strategies.
